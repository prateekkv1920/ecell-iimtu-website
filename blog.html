How InnoHack 1.0 Launched 3 Real Startups

When we launched InnoHack 1.0 back in January 2026, we had a simple goal: create a space where IIMT students could turn their ideas into something real in 24 hours. What happened exceeded every expectation we had.

120 students. 30 teams. 24 hours. The innovation lab buzzed with energy from 10 AM on January 15th to 10 AM on January 16th. Red Bull cans, whiteboard markers, and the smell of instant noodles filled the air.

The Winning Teams

1st Place — EduLeap (₹25,000 prize): Rahul Sharma and his team built a working prototype of an AI-powered adaptive learning platform in just 18 hours. The demo blew the judges away. EduLeap is now in active development with 500+ beta users.

2nd Place — FarmWallet (₹15,000 prize): Priya Singh's team identified a real problem — farmers couldn't access micro-loans quickly. Their digital wallet prototype connected farmers to lenders in under 5 minutes. FarmWallet is now piloting in 3 villages near Meerut.

3rd Place — HealthCircle (₹10,000 prize): Aryan Gupta's telemedicine concept connected rural patients to specialists via WhatsApp-based intake forms. Simple, elegant, impactful.

Key Lessons for Future Hackathon Participants

• Solve a real problem — don't build what's impressive, build what's needed.

• Demo > Deck — the teams that won showed working prototypes, not just slides.

• Speed of execution matters — teams that started coding by 10:30 AM did significantly better.
